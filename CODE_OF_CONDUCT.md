# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We expect all participants to adhere to this Code of Conduct in all interactions.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- Being respectful and inclusive of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Being constructive and professional in all communications
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Respecting the time and effort contributors put into the project
- Asking questions before making assumptions
- Assuming good intent in discussions

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment of any kind (personal attacks, trolling, etc.)
- Discriminatory language or actions based on race, gender, religion, sexuality, or other protected characteristics
- Unwelcome sexual attention or remarks
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Intentional disruption of discussions
- Aggressive or hostile behavior
- Spam or self-promotion

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All repository spaces (issues, pull requests, discussions, commits)
- Community channels (if any are created)
- Official events and communications
- Any space where you are representing the project

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience a violation of this Code of Conduct:

1. **Do not engage** with the person exhibiting harmful behavior
2. **Document the incident** with details and timestamps
3. **Report** to arslanbasharat.o.o@gmail.com
4. **Include**:
   - What happened (factually)
   - When it occurred
   - Who was involved
   - Links to relevant discussion/comments (if applicable)
   - Context you have

### Response

- Reports will be treated confidentially
- We will assess the situation objectively
- Actions may include warnings, temporary bans, or permanent removal
- Both parties will be notified of outcomes when applicable

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:
- Clarifying expectations for acceptable behavior
- Enforcing this Code of Conduct fairly and consistently
- Protecting the privacy of reporters
- Taking appropriate corrective action
